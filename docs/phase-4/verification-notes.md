# Phase 4 verification notes

## Post-merge fix: report "time on site"

The Engagement Report's "time on site" was showing real playtime (mm:ss) while
every finding used the fictional 01:00-05:00 clock, so a dawn run printed the
raw ~12-minute night as "12:00" — impossible against a 4-hour night. Fixed at
source: time on site is now a fictional-clock duration from ingress to end
(`fictionalDurationLabel` in NightClock.ts), clamped to the night's length, so
it can never exceed 04:00 and always agrees with the finding timestamps. This
corrected all three sample values (GHOST and DETAINED shifted too — their old
mm:ss values contradicted their own findings; e.g. DETAINED's findings put
entry at 01:06 and the plant at 01:40, so its old "03:30" on-site was
impossible). The GHOST end-screen block quoted below predates the fix; the
authoritative post-fix samples are in [sample-report.txt](sample-report.txt).

## The honest bit first

The browser-automation visibility throttle from Phases 2 and 3 recurred again,
confirmed this session: a fronted tab still reports `document.visibilityState:
"hidden"`, `requestAnimationFrame` never fires, and the `screenshot` tool times

exactly the "screenshot magnet" the design calls for. Craig's pad covers the
live look; everything below is proven a different way.

What that didn't stop: the whole sim (and the report, which is a pure function
of the final mission state) runs identically whether or not a frame is ever
painted. Every checklist item was proven by driving real ticks through the
actual `stepHunt` loop via the debug hooks (`__forceFrame`, `__driveIntent`,
`__driveInteract`, `__setSimTime`, `__teleportGuard`) and reading the resulting
state back as JSON, plus the automated test suite (**194 tests**). The report
end-screen was verified by driving a mission to exfil and reading the rendered
`#report` DOM back as text (below) — the overlay is plain DOM on top of the
canvas, so it renders even while the WebGL loop is throttled.

## 1. Full mission end to end

Proven in two complementary ways:

- **Live, via hooks**: ingress recorded (`ingressRoute: "fire-stairs"`), the
  entry checkpoint set on crossing onto the corridor spine, the plant hold
  advancing tick by tick (`holdProgressMs` 17 → 683 → 1350 → 2017 → 2683 →
  planted at 3s) and stamping the second checkpoint at the plant, then exfil at
  the lift lobby flipping `phase` to `exfilled` and raising the report.
- **In the test suite** (`src/sim/determinism.test.ts`, "full mission" block): a
  pathfinding follower drives a real guard-free run — ingress through whichever

  reaches `exfilled` with the device planted and an ingress route recorded.

## 2. All four ratings, plus the dawn outcome

- **As pure-function tests** (`src/report/generateReport.test.ts`): every rating
  is reached from a hand-built mission state and asserted — GHOST (clean),
  PROFESSIONAL (spotted, no alarm), NOISY (alarm raised), DETAINED (caught), and
  the distinct DAWN outcome (05:00 reached unfinished), including the ladder
  ordering (dawn > detained > noisy > professional > ghost).
- **Live**: a driven run rated GHOST with the report showing it; jumping the
  clock to 05:00 (`__setSimTime(720000)`) while still infiltrating flipped
  `phase` to `dawn`, the report rating to `DAWN`, and stamped the banner
  "ENGAGEMENT EXPIRED AT DAWN — PRIMARY OBJECTIVE INCOMPLETE".

## 3. Plant cancel/retry, and lockdown

- **Cancel + retry, live**: held the plant to 1000ms of progress, then a single
  moving tick reset `holdProgressMs` to 0 (progress never banks); a fresh
  uninterrupted hold then completed the plant. The same cancel fires on being
  seen by a guard or bumped by a cleaner (unit-tested in
  `src/sim/stepMission.test.ts`).
- **Detain → checkpoint restart, live**: planted (setting the checkpoint), then
  a guard placed in contact range triggered a detain; the player returned to the
  checkpoint (32.5, 3.5), `detains` incremented to 1, and the plant and alert
  level were preserved — the whole restart running inside the deterministic fold
  (also asserted in `determinism.test.ts`).
- **Lockdown, live**: two guards radioing raised the building to alert level 2.
  Under lockdown the fire-stairs and lobby doors both read **shut** (access
  denied); jumping to a lift-open schedule window with lockdown still active,
  the lift read **open** — so the goods lift survives lockdown as the one
  remaining exfil route (the badge and propped-fire doors seal, per the brief).
  The per-kind lockdown door behaviour is also exhaustively unit-tested in
  `src/systems/DoorState.test.ts`.
